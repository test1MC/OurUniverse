M6

This bright collection of about 80 stars are all moving through space together in The brightest star you see is a variable star, BM Scorpii, a yellow or orange supergiant that changes its magnitude between 5.5 and 7 with a semi-regular period. However, most of the stars here are hot, blue main sequence stars of spectral type B4-B5.

Members of this group were formed in the same giant molecular cloud and are still loosely gravitationally bound to each other. Studies have been conducted on Messier 6′s upper main sequence stars for strong, highly structured magnetic fields – leading researchers further into understanding the origins and evolution of Ap stars in open clusters.an area spanning about 12 to 25 light years across – and may have formed anywhere from 51 to 95 million years ago.
